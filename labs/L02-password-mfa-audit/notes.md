# L02 Notes - Password + MFA Audit

## What I Practiced

In this lab, I practiced reviewing account security settings and identifying risks related to passwords and multi-factor authentication.

I focused on:

- Password uniqueness
- Password strength
- MFA status
- MFA method
- Recovery options
- Login alerts
- Risk reduction

---

## Safety Rules I Followed

I did not document:

- Actual passwords
- Usernames
- Email addresses
- Phone numbers
- Recovery codes
- Backup codes
- Authenticator QR codes
- Security question answers

---

## How I Reviewed Each Account

For each account, I asked:

1. Is the password unique?
2. Is the password strong?
3. Is MFA enabled?
4. What type of MFA is being used?
5. Are recovery options updated?
6. Are login alerts available?
7. What could happen if this account was compromised?
8. What improvement would reduce the risk?

---

## What I Learned

I learned that the primary email account is especially important because it can often be used to reset passwords for other accounts.

I also learned that MFA is a layered security control. Even if a password is stolen, MFA can make it harder for an attacker to access the account.
