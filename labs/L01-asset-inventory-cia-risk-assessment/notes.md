# L01 Notes - Asset Inventory + CIA Risk Assessment

## What I Practiced

In this lab, I practiced identifying technology assets and thinking through the risks connected to each one.

I focused on the CIA Triad:

- Confidentiality
- Integrity
- Availability

---

## How I Thought Through Each Asset

For each asset, I asked:

1. What is this asset?
2. What type of asset is it?
3. What data might be stored or processed there?
4. What could happen if the wrong person accessed it?
5. What could happen if the data was changed?
6. What could happen if the asset became unavailable?
7. What protection would reduce the risk?

---

## CIA Triad Breakdown

### Confidentiality

Confidentiality focuses on keeping sensitive information private and preventing unauthorized access.

Examples from this lab include:

- Employee records in the HR database
- Customer data in the customer database
- Emails and attachments on the email server
- Backup data on the backup drive

### Integrity

Integrity focuses on keeping data accurate, complete, and trustworthy.

Examples from this lab include:

- Payroll records being changed incorrectly
- Customer records being modified
- Firewall rules being changed without approval
- Backup files becoming corrupted

### Availability

Availability focuses on making sure systems and data are accessible when needed.

Examples from this lab include:

- Employees needing laptops to complete work
- Email being available for communication
- Wi-Fi being available for connectivity
- Backups being available during recovery

---

## What I Learned

I learned that cybersecurity starts with knowing what needs to be protected. If an organization does not know what assets it has, it cannot fully understand its risk.

I also learned that one asset can have confidentiality, integrity, and availability risks at the same time.

For example, a backup drive creates:
- A confidentiality risk if it is stolen
- An integrity risk if the backup is corrupted
- An availability risk if it fails during recovery
